# 🌌 Overview: Extended Unified Theory (EUT)

Extended Unified Theory (EUT) is a research framework that starts from a simple philosophical and mathematical stance: **resonance before form**.

Rather than assuming fixed spacetime structure first and fitting everything else into it, EUT explores whether coherent harmonic organization can come first, with familiar geometry and matter appearing as emergent outcomes.

## Core Ideas in Plain Language

### 1) Resonance before form

EUT treats harmonic organization as primary. Stable patterns of resonance are taken as the root layer from which higher-level structure can be derived.

### 2) Geometry as emergent

In this view, geometry is not always fundamental. Instead, geometric objects (metric, connection, curvature behavior) can emerge from an underlying harmonic field through a map from field structure to effective geometry.

### 3) Matter as stable harmonic structure

Matter-like behavior is approached as persistent, localized, or topologically protected harmonic configurations. In practice, this means looking for robust modes that remain coherent under perturbation and can play the role of physical degrees of freedom.

### 4) Information and thermodynamics as coherence structure

EUT also treats informational and thermodynamic behavior through coherence and decoherence patterns. Entropy-like quantities, horizon behavior, and entanglement-like relations are studied as organization properties of the same harmonic substrate.

## Why this repository exists

EUT-Core is designed to keep this work transparent and modular: theory notes, computational tests, manuscripts, and references are organized so others can inspect assumptions, reproduce demonstrations, and contribute improvements.
